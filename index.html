Why do essential workers need help?

If you have not read, Learn more, I would encourage you to do so. As you can see these people are in dire need of help for their day to day activities. These people are on the edge of survival. That is why we need your help.

How will these donations benefit an essential worker?

The aim of these donations is to supply these essential workers with some funds to be able to afford their next meal, contribute to their rent, enable them to buy personal protective equipment and to take care of themselves due to their position in this pandemic.

When will essential workers be receiving the donations?

As soon as you have made a donation, the funds will be sent to our applicants, who have applied directly, and will be distributed in a just manner to all applicants.


Who are the essential workers, which we are helping?

The Food Industry -Businesses that primarily sell food, beverages and consumer products necessary to maintain households and businesses like grocery stores, retailers and restaurants

Services - Primarily that focus on customer service like pharmacies, healthcare, retail workers, community services, child care services, Health care and social services

Agricultural and food production - Businesses that produce food and beverages, and agricultural products including plants, including farming, harvesting, aquaculture, hunting and fishing.

We will not be providing help to supply chains, financial services, telecommunications providers, Transportation services, manufacturing, Construction, sports, research, Resource and energy - We see the people working under these sectors as a responsibility of their employers.

What are the struggles of these essential worker?

The UFCW had major chains, which prohibited workers from wearing face masks and gloves while working, lift those restrictions, with some workers gaining hazard pay but the amount is insignificant. Most employees request personal protective equipment (hand sanitizer and disinfectant wipes). Not all the demands of the employees are being met.

Ride-hail drivers are having a massive decline in customers and Shift workers have lost their jobs and/or had their shifts cut. These people have requested unemployment benefits, but that is being declined due to the lack of legal clarity (ride-hail drivers) or their benefits do not cover their living costs (Service workers)

The UFCW has also supported Healthcare services but there is a lack of paid time off and no paid sick days, this lead to more protests.

Agricultural industries are at risk due to them running on skeleton staff, again placing a massive strain on the food supply. Unlike the retail workers the United farm workers union has tried to provide workers with sick pay to 40 hours or more to maintain the food supply belt.

In-home supportive service workers have a low hourly pay and only 9% get employee-sponsored healthcare. Many of them work with people who are immune-compromised. Domestic workers, are asked by their employers to not return to work, as there is fear of spreading the virus, but since many who are employed are not legally employed, they cannot apply for unemployment insurance. Neither of these services is provided with personal protection equipment.
